# Pijon Lore



&#x09;Long time ago the Pijones were the masters of the skies, living in perfect harmony and traveling together as a peaceful, unbreakable flock. But this era of light was shattered by a tragic accident. A young Pijon was exploring the depths of the overworld, when it stumbled upon a strange ancient structure. Its curiosity took the better of it and this resulted in it slipping through an active portal, becoming trapped in the freezing, desolate dimension of the End. The gateway closed forever behind it. With no seeds or water, the Pijon's only source of survival was the Chorus Fruits that grew on the barren islands of the Void. Over hundreds of years, this strange diet, combined with its only company being the Endermen and the corrupted magic of the dimension, twisted its body and mind. Its feathers turned black and grew to an immense size. Its eyes began to glow a piercing purple, and its gentle cooing was replaced by a deafening, terrifying roar. The little Pijon was gone and in its place stood the Ender Dragon, condemned to fly eternally in an empty, dark sky, driven entirely to madness.



&#x09;Centuries later, the ancient flock finally uncovered the dark truth about their lost sibling. They had never forgotten the missing Pijon, since it was part of their family. It was one of them or at least, it used to be. Instead of fleeing in fear, the Pijones united once more. With the help and guidance of the humans who had brought them all together again, the flock despite the differences some had developed from living in various biomes, stepped through the portal with a single, heartbreaking purpose. To put the dragon out of its misery and finally grant it peace. They knew the only way to free it from the curse of the End was death.



&#x09;Yet, when the Pijones saw what had become of the twisted creature, their sibling, they were paralyzed by shock and heartbreak. Unable to deliver the any kind of attack themselves, they left the grim task to the humans. Strangely, even though the Pijones were there with the humans, that were to end its life, the terrifying dragon did not attack them. A miracle occurred. Every time it looked upon the small, feathered creatures, deeply buried memories awakened within its clouded, maddened mind. It remembered the warmth of the sun, the wind beneath its old wings, and the unconditional, eternal love of its flock. This lingering spark of its past broke through the corruption. The dragon lowered its defenses, accepting its end as the ultimate redemption, finding peace in the knowledge that it was dying beside its own family, a family that had traveled to the very edge of the world to bring its soul finally some peace and release it from centuries of pain and suffering.



&#x09;Just as the humans prepared to finish it off, something occured. They ran out of arrows, and their weapons shattered. Defenseless, they had to act fast before the dragon's corrupted instincts took over and killed them first. Out of sheer desperation, they gathered the Pijones’ poop and threw it at the End Dragon. What they did not expect was the massive damage it dealt to the dragon. Over the passing years, the dark magic of its transformation had left the dragon fatally vulnerable to the unique chemicals found in the Pijones' poop. And so, that was how everything finally ended. The mighty Ender Dragon was pooped to death. They had finally succeeded, freeing the poor soul of the Ender Dragon that was once just one little Pijon.
